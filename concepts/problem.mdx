---
title: "Problem"
description: "Why browsing leaks data and why ZK verification needs better rails"
---

Traditional browsing exposes user data through trackers, logins, and CAPTCHAs, leading to privacy breaches and forced data sharing with websites.

At the same time, verifying ZK proofs on common chains can be expensive:

- Typical verification costs on Ethereum can be **200k–300k gas per proof**, reaching **$60+** during congestion.
- Not all proof systems (e.g. STARKs from zkVMs) are efficiently verifiable on the EVM.

Users need a seamless way to prove claims like **“human”** or **“over 18”** without revealing underlying data. Websites need **trustworthy, chain-agnostic attestations** that don’t require bespoke ZK integrations.
